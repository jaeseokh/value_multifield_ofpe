4 Figure 2: Limitations of tree ensembles for a multi-year field

Here we focus on a single farm–field with multiple years of OFPE trials. The figure compares observed yield to predictions from a tree-based ensemble model (for example, random forest or XGBoost) trained on all other locations and evaluated in a leave-one-location-out or leave-one-year-out fashion.

The key message is that, under the current data quantity and quality, purely data-driven models struggle to generalize yield predictions to new field-year conditions.

# Figure 3: Yield–N response comparison (true vs ML forecasts)

For the same field, we compare yield–N response curves:

an ex-post “true” yield–N curve derived from the observed strip-level trials, and

one or more forecast curves obtained by aggregating machine learning predictions across N rates.

This figure illustrates that even when we step back from point-level prediction and focus on yield–N curves, the ML models do not reliably recover the response with the current data.
